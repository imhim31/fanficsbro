"So what are you going to do?" Butcher asks, still incredulous.

"I wanted to talk through that with you, actually," Homelander says. He's not smiling, his face is pretty unreadable, and Butcher has no idea what there is to discuss.

"Could we take this to the living room? Sorry, I need tea. Seeing you lumbering around in the dark parched me out, I'll admit."

Homelander shrugs and walks past Butcher out of the room.

As Butcher boils water he keeps glancing over at the bizarre sight of Homelander sitting at the table in civilian clothing—his own clothing, no less—busy examining his grown out fingernails with some disdain.

"There now." Butcher sits down with a teacup in a chair across from Homelander's. "What did you want to discuss, love?" It's probably needlessly cheeky to speak this way to the supe who could laser his brains out any second, but Butcher can't help it at this point.

"I've thought this through a lot, but I need buy-in from you." Homelander probably notices Butcher's quizzical look and clarifies. "Thought a lot about what I'd do once I managed to free myself. Who are my friends and who are my enemies, so to speak. As far as I'm concerned, I don't ever want to be entangled with a company that tried to kill me, and I don't want them near Ryan either. And I like you a lot, William. I'd say I'm in love with you, whether you feel that way or not."

"Don't say that." Butcher grimaces. "Please don't say that. You've been isolated and—"

"I'll say what I want. That's one benefit of not being imprisoned by you anymore. But I'll also say, what I want is to be in a partnership with you, and I'm willing to follow your rules if that's what makes you happy."

"What partnership..."

Homelander sighs, as if a little bit exasperated with having to elaborate, but proceeds. "I want to raise Ryan together. I want to continue what we had here. But it'll only work if you take a compound V injection."

"Yeah, sorry, hard pass right there. That's the last—"

"I knew you wouldn't like the idea, but hear me out." Cunt really has mulled this conversation over in his head, apparently. "I can think of at least four reasons you might be interested. Reason one, you think I'm inherently rotten, that my personality is going to lead me to always do the wrong things. I don't agree but we can have that argument another time. I'm offering you a good way to ensure that you can keep me in check. I value your opinion enough that I don't want you to be angry with me, and I'm offering you a way to even the playing field physically."

"If I survive the injection at all—"

"Reason two," Homelander talks right through Butcher's asides. "Now that I know some mundane anticorrosive coating any Joe Schmoe can order online depowers me, you'll understand that I'm a little more nervous about my ability to protect myself and Ryan from Vought. You don't seem affected by this zinc molybdate shit—yes, I took a look around the shed already—so that's my hedge against Vought and whoever they sell or gift this intel to. If you take the V, you can certainly protect Ryan better."

"You're the one who's convinced we can't hide him from Vought, not me."

"Reason three, you've wanted to take down Vought. Now I want to take it down too. I think it makes a lot of sense to do it together."

"And how are we doing that?"

"Beyond the scope here, but I have some ideas, trust me. But, again, I need someone powerful to have my back if I have weaknesses they know about."

Finally there's a pause in the one-sided conversation.

"And what's your fourth brilliant reason?" Butcher asks, sipping down the last bit of tea in his cup.

Homelander looks askance at him before smiling. "I had a lot of fun the day before yesterday. I assume the feeling is mutual. No reason for that to have been a one-off."

Butcher is trying to digest everything this cunt is proposing, and finally nods. "And what happens if I refuse? What are you threatening me with?"

Homelander surprises Butcher with how dramatically he sighs and throws up his arms. "Nothing, alright?! I don't want to threaten you, William. But tell me if you have a better idea of how to raise and protect my son. Our son. He's yours too."

Butcher considers saying 'keep you far away from him' but bites it back. Everything about this middle-of-the-night negotiation feels surreal. It's bizarre seeing Homelander in his own clothing, proposing to enter into some fucked up union and asking Butcher to corrupt himself permanently into everything he's hated. But he has to admit there's some logic in it, and he can't think clearly enough to point out flaws.

"Where are you planning to get Compound V if you're not going back to being Vought’s lapdog?"

"Don't worry about that, I have caches of it."

"I'm sure they've liquidated all your assets by now."

"I'm telling you, don't worry about it. Fuck it, you don't have to take my word for it." Homelander gets up abruptly and walks out the door. Before Butcher can understand what he's doing, he hears the familiar sound of this supe taking off full speed into the air.

"Goddammit." Butcher's not sure where the supe decided to fly off to, but he needs to think fast about whether there's anything he can do while Homelander’s away. If he injects himself with Temp V right this minute, could he fight him and put him back in the cage? The cage is destroyed, and he doesn't seem to have much ZM on hand here. There's melted puddles of white metal burned into the floor all around the living room—Mallory's going to be pissed about how this house looks—but none of that is going to be useful.

He doesn't get very far in his thoughts before he hears Homelander land, already returned.

"Nice little town we've been staying in. Not familiar enough with towns in west Jersey to know it by name," he comments as he walks back in. Butcher can't help but be impressed with what must be some photographic memory or biological GPS this supe possesses.

There's a vial of the cursed blue liquid in Homelander's hand, as well as a few syringes. "Well here it is. Told you not to worry about it."

"I'll think about it," Butcher replies flatly, realizing with some discomfort that there's nothing really stopping Homelander from forcing an injection on him, but all the more intent on acting calm because of that.

"How long are you planning to think about it?"

"Are you in some sort of rush?" Butcher asks.

Homelander purses his lips but shakes his head dismissively.

"Let me sleep on it. I can't think clearly in the middle of the night."

"Fine," Homelander says, shrugging, and Butcher feels a strange combination of dread and amusement when the supe trails behind and follows him into the bedroom. Butcher opens the blinds but turns off the light. He watches Homelander taking off everything but the boxers and getting in on one side of the bed.

"You're gonna sleep here?" he asks, getting in on the other side.

"Am I not welcome?" Homelander fires back.

Butcher shrugs. They lie face to face, staring at each other.

"If I bring you to Ryan, will you tell him what I've done with you here?" Butcher asks.

"What do you think. Unless you've already told him what happened between me and Becca..."

"I haven't. Although I don't like lies. Mostly to protect him from thinking he's some kind of unwanted child."

"Well, then I'll protect him from knowing what Uncle Billy likes to get up to in his spare time."

Butcher sighs. "What're you going to tell him about why you're back from the dead?"

"Same thing I'm going to tell the press. Back from my mission, too much PTSD to talk about it, and start stirring the pot about why I'm not going back to work under Vought."

Butcher seriously doubts he's going to be able to fall back asleep with all the thoughts racing through his head, but they lie in silence for a while, just staring at each other in the moonlit room.

"Are you going to change your name if it's a Vought trademark and you're separating from them?" Butcher asks, suddenly curious.

"To what."

"I don't know, I was wondering if you'd given that any thought."

"I'll have to sleep on it," Homelander replies, turning over and facing away.
